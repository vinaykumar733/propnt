01 — Definitions

The Lexicon

"Discovery"

The phase where users interact with anonymized property signals through conversational AI, without access to identifying details.

"Intent Qualification"

The process of verifying user seriousness through structured questions, document submission, and timeline confirmation.

"StayBroker"

Verified human mediators who facilitate controlled introductions between qualified seekers and verified providers.

"Provider"

Property owners, operators, or lessors who have submitted verification documents and listed supply signals.

"Seeker"

Individuals or entities searching for residential property, business acquisition, or lease opportunities.

"Silo Protocol"

The technical architecture preventing direct contact between parties until mediation checkpoints are cleared.

03 — The Protocol Rules

Non-Negotiables

PropNT functions only if these rules are universally observed. Violation constitutes breach and grounds for immediate termination.

No Bypass Attempts

Any attempt to circumvent StayBroker mediation—extracting contact details from conversation, using partial information to locate properties independently, or soliciting direct contact before qualification—is prohibited. We monitor for pattern-based bypass attempts.

No Information Extraction

Users may not scrape, harvest, or systematically collect property signals, location data, or conversation patterns. Our supply layer is proprietary and access is personal, not transferable.

No Misrepresentation

False statements during qualification—budget, timeline, identity, purpose—void all protections. We verify selectively but thoroughly. Discovered misrepresentation results in permanent exclusion and potential legal action.

Confidentiality of Mediation

Communications through StayBroker are confidential. Disclosing mediator identities, conversation content, or negotiation details to third parties breaches the trust layer and terminates access.

05 — StayBroker Relationship

Mediation, Not Intermediation

StayBroker mediators are not agents of either party. They are process controllers with a singular mandate: convert qualified intent into structured transaction opportunities.

Mediators may:

  • → Verify documents and identity claims
  • → Structure initial conversations and expectations
  • → Facilitate physical or virtual property access
  • → Document negotiation progress and outcomes
  • → Terminate mediation if protocol violations occur

Mediators do not:

  • → Represent either party in legal or financial matters
  • → Guarantee transaction outcomes or property condition
  • → Accept fees from both parties simultaneously (conflict protocol)
